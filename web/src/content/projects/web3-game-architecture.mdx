This was where I cut my teeth on blockchain at depth. Over a multi-year engagement I designed, and redesigned no fewer than six times, the architecture for a web3 game, spanning the whole stack: protocol, backend services, frontend, and the token contracts themselves.

Each redesign came from something real: new scale, new requirements, or a security posture that had to get stricter. Working across every layer meant I couldn't hand-wave any of it: enforcement, account abstraction, and Sign-In with Ethereum all had to fit together and hold.

The client operates stealth, so I keep the specifics general. What carried forward is the judgment: EVM systems reward people who understand the whole path from contract to interface, and punish those who don't.
